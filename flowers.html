WATER LILY

Water lilies are rooted in soil in bodies of water, with leaves and flowers floating on or emergent from the surface.

There are about seventy different types of water lilies.

DAHILA


This plants native to Mexico and Central America. A member of the Compositae (also called Asteraceae).
The dahlia was declared the national flower of Mexico in 1963.
There are 42 species of dahlia.



PEONY


Peonies are native to Asia, Europe and Western North America.
There are about 33 different types of peony.
The flowers have a short blooming season, usually only 7–10 days.



ALSTROEMERIA


Alstroemeria commonly called the Peruvian lily or lily of the Incas. They are all native to South America, although some have become naturalized in the United States, Mexico, Australia...
There are about seventy different types of water lilies.



DAISY


The daisy family is also known as the aster family, or Asteraceae.
There are 23,000 individual species in this family. The name “daisy” is based on the words “day’s eye”, probably because the head opens in the morning and closes each night.



LOTUS


Nelumbo is a genus of aquatic plants with large, showy flowers. Members are commonly called lotus.
Members outwardly resemble those in the family Nymphaeaceae ("water lilies"), but Nelumbo is actually very distant to that family.


CALLA


Calla (bog arum, marsh calla, wild calla, squaw claw, and water-arum) is a genus of flowering plant in the family Araceae.
It is native to cool temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, in central, eastern and northern Europe (France and Norway eastward), northern Asia and northern North America.


BIRD OF PARADISE


Strelitzia is a genus of five species of perennial plants, native to South Africa. Common name of the genus is bird of paradise flower/plant, because of a resemblance of its flowers to birds-of-paradise.



CARNATION


Dianthus caryophyllus, commonly known as the carnation or clove pink, is a species of Dianthus.
It is likely native to the Mediterranean region but its exact range is unknown due to extensive cultivation for the last 2,000 years.



TULIP


The tulip is a member of the lily family, Liliaceae.
There are about 75 species, and these are divided among four subgenera. The name "tulip" is thought to be derived from a Persian word for turban.



ORCHID


Orchids are plants that belong to the family Orchidaceae. The family encompasses about 6–11% of all species of seed plants.
The Orchidaceae have about 28,000 currently accepted species, distributed in about 763 genera.
